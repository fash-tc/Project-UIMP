Section 03

Alert Severity Model & Response Tiers

Not every alert is an incident, and not every incident requires the same response. The platform routes alerts through a four-tier severity model with context-aware behavior that adapts to business hours vs. on-call periods.

A Note on On-Call and Response Times A well-instrumented alerting platform with proper aggregation, correlation, and automated paging does not produce slower response times when the SRE team is on-call vs. when they are sitting at their desks. The entire purpose of this platform is to ensure that the right alert, with the right context, reaches the right person within seconds — regardless of whether that person is in the office, at home, or asleep. An on-call SRE receiving a P1 page with correlated alerts, LLM analysis, linked dashboards, and a suggested runbook will begin effective investigation faster than a team of engineers staring at six disconnected monitoring dashboards waiting for something to turn red. More eyes on screens is not a substitute for proper tooling and monitoring aggregation. Investing in the tooling described in this document is what drives MTTD and MTTR down — not staffing levels during off-hours. The data will prove this: we will measure TTA and MTTR across business-hours and on-call periods to demonstrate that the platform delivers consistent response times regardless of operational mode.

Four-Tier Severity Model

P1 — Critical

Active customer impact. Service outage or severe degradation affecting domain registration, DNS resolution, registry operations, or data integrity. Revenue-impacting.

Who is paged: SRE + Engineering

Automated actions:
✓ Keep correlates and groups alerts
✓ GoAlert pages SRE + Engineering on-call (SMS + voice)
✓ n8n creates dedicated Slack incident channel
✓ LLM analysis generated and posted
✓ Jira incident ticket created automatically
✓ Status page updated
✓ Leadership notified via #incidents-p1
✓ During business hours: Slack notification replaces paging for on-desk teams

P2 — Major

Likely customer impact. Significant degradation, partial outage, elevated error rates across a service, failover triggered, redundancy lost.

Who is paged: SRE + Engineering

Automated actions:
✓ Keep correlates and groups alerts
✓ GoAlert pages SRE + Engineering on-call
✓ Posted to team-specific Slack channel
✓ Jira incident ticket created automatically
✓ LLM analysis generated and posted
✗ No incident channel unless manually declared
✗ No status page update unless manually triggered
✓ During business hours: Slack notification replaces paging for on-desk teams

P3 — Medium

Potential or minor impact. Replication lag, resource nearing capacity, single-node degradation with redundancy intact, elevated but non-critical error rates.

Who is paged: SRE only

Automated actions:
✓ Keep correlates and groups alerts
✓ GoAlert pages SRE on-call (SMS)
✓ Posted to team-specific Slack channel
✓ Jira incident ticket created automatically
✗ No engineering paging
✗ No incident channel
✗ No status page update
○ SRE resolves or escalates to engineering manually
✓ During business hours: Slack notification only — no page

P4 — Informational

Non-impacting. Disk usage trends, certificate expiry > 30 days, non-critical service health, maintenance reminders, capacity planning data points.

Who is paged: Nobody

Automated actions:
✓ Logged in Keep for tracking and trending
✓ Posted to #alerts-noise channel
✗ No paging — ever
✗ No Jira ticket
✗ No individual notifications
○ SRE triages in batch during next business day

Business Hours vs. On-Call Behavior

The platform adapts its notification strategy based on whether the SRE team is working (business hours) or on-call (evenings, weekends, holidays). This is not about reducing response quality during on-call — it's about using the right notification channel for the context.

SeverityBusiness Hours (Team at Desk)On-Call (Evenings / Weekends)
P1 Slack channel notification + Slack DM to team leads. No SMS/voice page needed — team is already at their desks and monitoring Slack. Incident channel still created. Jira ticket created. Full GoAlert paging: SMS + voice call to SRE + Engineering on-call. Incident channel created. Jira ticket created. LLM analysis. Status page.
P2 Slack channel notification + Slack DM. No page. Jira ticket created. SRE investigates from desk. SMS + voice page to SRE + Engineering on-call. Jira ticket created. LLM analysis.
P3 Slack channel notification only. Jira ticket created. SRE picks up during regular workflow. SMS page to SRE on-call only. Jira ticket created. No engineering notification.
P4 Slack #alerts-noise. Triaged in batch. Slack #alerts-noise. No page. No notification. Handled next business day.
How Business Hours Are Determined n8n workflows check a schedule configuration (a simple JSON object or GoAlert schedule API) before deciding whether to trigger GoAlert paging or Slack-only notification. This is not hardcoded — it respects holidays, schedule overrides, and timezone for distributed team members. If the schedule says someone is on-desk, Slack is sufficient. If the schedule says on-call, GoAlert pages via SMS/voice. The same alert, same severity, same response — just a different notification channel.

Jira Integration — Automatic Incident Ticketing

For P1, P2, and P3 alerts, the platform automatically creates a Jira incident ticket via n8n workflow. This ensures every significant alert has a tracking artifact, even if it is resolved quickly by the SRE team without engineering involvement.

When the incident is resolved in Keep, n8n updates the Jira ticket status automatically and attaches resolution notes.

Keep Workflow — Severity-Based Routing

workflow: id: severity-routing description: Route alerts by severity with business-hours awareness triggers: - type: alert actions: # ── P1 — Critical ────────────────────────── - name: p1-slack-notify if: "'{{ alert.severity }}' == 'critical'" provider: type: slack config: "{{ providers.slack-incidents }}" with: message: "🔴 *P1 CRITICAL* — {{ alert.name }} ..." - name: p1-page-oncall # n8n checks business hours if: "'{{ alert.severity }}' == 'critical'" provider: type: webhook with: url: "https://n8n.internal/webhook/severity-router" body: '{"severity":"P1","alert":"{{ alert }}"}' # n8n decides: GoAlert page (on-call) or Slack DM (business hours) # n8n also creates Jira ticket + incident channel # ── P2 — Major ───────────────────────────── - name: p2-slack-notify if: "'{{ alert.severity }}' == 'high'" provider: type: slack config: "{{ providers.slack-team-channel }}" with: message: "🟠 *P2 MAJOR* — {{ alert.name }} ..." - name: p2-page-and-ticket if: "'{{ alert.severity }}' == 'high'" provider: type: webhook with: url: "https://n8n.internal/webhook/severity-router" body: '{"severity":"P2","alert":"{{ alert }}"}' # ── P3 — Medium ──────────────────────────── - name: p3-slack-notify if: "'{{ alert.severity }}' == 'warning'" provider: type: slack config: "{{ providers.slack-team-channel }}" with: message: "🟡 *P3 MEDIUM* — {{ alert.name }} — SRE investigating" - name: p3-page-sre-and-ticket if: "'{{ alert.severity }}' == 'warning'" provider: type: webhook with: url: "https://n8n.internal/webhook/severity-router" body: '{"severity":"P3","alert":"{{ alert }}"}' # ── P4 — Informational ───────────────────── - name: p4-noise-channel if: "'{{ alert.severity }}' in ['info', 'low']" provider: type: slack config: "{{ providers.slack-noise }}" with: message: "🔵 *P4 INFO* — {{ alert.name }}"
The n8n Severity Router — The Intelligence Layer All P1–P3 alerts route through an n8n webhook (/webhook/severity-router) that acts as the decision engine. It checks: (1) Is the affected team currently in business hours or on-call? (2) Based on severity + schedule, should this be a GoAlert page or Slack-only? (3) Create the Jira ticket with appropriate priority. (4) For P1: also create the incident channel, trigger LLM analysis, and update the status page. This single n8n workflow is the "brain" that adapts alert behavior to context.

Recommended Model Stack

Runbook Matching
